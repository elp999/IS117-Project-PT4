What foods are unhealthy

Common unhealthy foods often include those high in added sugars, refined grains, unhealthy fats, and sodium. Processed snacks like potato chips, cookies, and sugary cereals are typically laden with added sugars, unhealthy fats, and artificial ingredients. Fast food items such as burgers, fries, and fried chicken tend to be high in trans fats, sodium, and calories, contributing to weight gain and increased risk of chronic diseases like heart disease and diabetes. Sugary beverages like soda, energy drinks, and sweetened juices provide empty calories and can lead to weight gain, tooth decay, and insulin resistance. Additionally, desserts like cakes, pastries, and ice cream are rich in refined sugars and unhealthy fats, offering little to no nutritional value while promoting overconsumption of calories. Regular consumption of these unhealthy foods can have detrimental effects on overall health and well-being.

Fatty Liver Disease
High fructose corn syrup can lead to fatty liver disease.
May cause liver inflammation and scarring.
May increase risk of liver failure and liver cancer.
Leptin Resistance
High fructose corn syrup can cause leptin resistance.
May disrupt hunger signals and lead to overeating.
